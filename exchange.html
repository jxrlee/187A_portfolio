raveXchange

August 2011

Front-end by Joseph Lee. Back-end by David Yoo.

RaveXchange is a site for buying, selling, and trading event tickets. The idea originated in early 2011 when there were too many scalpers and scammers on Craigslist.

Landing Page

I started with a minimalistic landing page inspired by Tumblr's very own home page. Here users would be able to sign up or login. I decided to leave out any sort of call to action text or images because the site was still in the beta stages. This way most sign ups will be from referral and word of mouth.

Marketplace

The marketplace is the most important page of the site because all event tickets are listed here. The page continues with the minimalist theme set by the landing page. It uses distinct sections to break up the page and a fixed sidebar for quick navigation. Tickets can be filtered by location and sorted by recent and price. The top provides information on the event and a live twitter feed of what people are saying about it. Clicking on Sell takes users to a short form to fill.
